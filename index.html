Clinton Enwerem

I am a second-year Electrical & Computer Engineering Ph.D. student at the University of Maryland, College Park, MD, USA, advised by Professor John S. Baras, Distinguished University Professor and Endowed Lockheed Martin Chair in Systems Engineering. My research interests lie broadly at the nexus of robotics, control theory, and machine learning. At UMD, I am affiliated with the Institute for Systems Research and the Autonomy, Robotics, & Cognition Lab.

Before coming to UMD, I worked as a research assistant in the Control & Instrumentation Lab at my alma mater with Ihechiluru Okoro on topics spanning robust control, observer-based compensator design, and control of time-delayed systems.

Working under the supervision of Udoka Nwaneto, I earned my Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Nigeria, where I specialized in Control Systems and Electric Drives.

Research

My current work seeks to explore the interplay between control theory, optimization, and machine learning, and how we can merge well-developed techniques from these areas to solve open problems in robot autonomy. Specifically, I am keen on investigating the application of modern control-theoretic and data-driven machine learning methods in focus areas such as multiagent decision-making and safety-critical control for mobile robotic agents.

Robust Control of a DC motor
Ihechiluru Okoro, Clinton Enwerem
Heliyon, 2020

Design and simulation of robust and setpoint-tracking DC electric-drive controllers, which yield effective regulatory performance markedly superior to conventional control methods.
