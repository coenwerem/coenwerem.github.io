Clinton Enwerem

I am a first-year Electrical & Computer Engineering Ph.D. student at the University of Maryland, College Park, MD, USA, advised by Professor John S. Baras, Distinguished University Professor and Endowed Lockheed Martin Chair in Systems Engineering. My research interests lie broadly at the nexus of robotics, control theory, and machine learning. At UMD, I am also affiliated with the Institute for Systems Research and the Autonomy, Robotics, & Cognition Lab.

Before coming to UMD, I worked as a research assistant with Ihechiluru Okoro, MSc on topics spanning robust control, observer-based compensator design, and control of time-delayed systems.

I earned my Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Nigeria under the supervision of Udoka Nwaneto, MSc, EIT, where I specialized in Control Systems and Electric Drives.

Research

I am currently exploring the interplay between control theory, optimization, and machine learning, and how we can merge well-developed techniques from these areas to solve open problems in robot autonomy. Specifically, I am keen on investigating the application of modern control-theoretic methods in focus areas such as mobile robotics and robot manipulation, on the motion-planning and decision-making fronts.
