Bio

I am a second-year Electrical & Computer Engineering Ph.D. student at the University of Maryland (UMD), College Park (CP), MD, USA, advised by Professor John S. Baras, Distinguished University Professor and Endowed Lockheed Martin Chair in Systems Engineering. My research interests lie broadly at the nexus of control theory, multiagent systems, and robotics. At UMD, I am affiliated with the Institute for Systems Research, the Systems Engineering & Integration Lab, and the Autonomy, Robotics, & Cognition Lab.

Before UMD

Prior to resuming doctoral studies at UMD, I completed a year-long stint as a robotics trainee at Nigeria's foremost robotics and AI research center — Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Nigeria (RAIN). At RAIN, I worked with Dr. Olusola Ayoola on a number of robotics projects spanning robot navigation, visual SLAM, and robot control. Before that, in affiliation with the Electrical Engineering Department at my alma mater, I collaborated with Ihechiluru Okoro on research topics at the intersection of robust control, observer-based compensator design, and feedback control of time-delayed systems.

Undergrad

I earned my undergraduate degree in Electrical Engineering (with First Class Honors) from the University of Nigeria, where I worked on model-based speed control techniques for electric drives, under the supervision of Dr. Udoka Nwaneto.

Research

I work in the general areas of control theory, multiagent systems, and robotics. On the whole, my research is centered around the coordination and control of networked (engineered and human-robot) multiagent systems in dynamic environments. In particular, my current work is focused on topics such as formation control, consensus, multiagent decision-making, and safety-critical control for interacting agents, with applications to robotics.
