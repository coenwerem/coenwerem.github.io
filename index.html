Clinton Enwerem (クリントン)

I am a second-year Electrical & Computer Engineering Ph.D. student at the University of Maryland, College Park, MD, USA, advised by Professor John S. Baras, Distinguished University Professor and Endowed Lockheed Martin Chair in Systems Engineering. My research interests lie broadly at the nexus of robotics, control theory, and multiagent systems. At UMD, I am affiliated with the Institute for Systems Research and the Autonomy, Robotics, & Cognition Lab.

Prior to resuming doctoral studies at UMD, I completed a year-long stint as a robotics trainee at Nigeria's foremost robotics and AI research center — Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Nigeria (RAIN). At RAIN, I worked with Dr. Olusola Ayoola on diverse robotics projects spanning robot navigation, visual SLAM, and robot control. Before that, I worked with Ihechiluru Okoro as a research assistant in the Control & Instrumentation Lab — at my alma mater — on topics at the intersection of robust control, observer-based compensator design, and control of time-delayed systems.

Working under the supervision of Udoka Nwaneto, I earned my Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Nigeria, where I specialized in Control Systems and Electric Drives.

Research

I work in the general areas of control theory, multiagent systems, and robotics. On the whole, my research is centered around the coordination and control of networked (engineered and human-robot) multiagent systems in dynamic environments. In particular, my work is focused on topics such as formation control, consensus, multiagent decision-making, and safety-critical control for interacting mobile robotic agents.
