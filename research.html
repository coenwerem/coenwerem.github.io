Research

I study the coordination and control (at the agent and ensemble levels) of networked, intelligent, embodied, and possibly heterogeneous collectives in dynamic and partially-observed environments, using mathematical tools from graph theory, control theory, learning theory, and optimization. My focus is on developing techniques for safe and robust autonomy under uncertainty, with specific focus on problems spanning robust reinforcement learning, trajectory planning, and safety-critical control, drawing examples from the robotics literature and related fields. In the past, I have worked on problems in formation tracking, dynamic consensus, and safety-critical control synthesis for multiagent systems.
